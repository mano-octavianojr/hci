Human-Computer Interaction

  • Instructor: Manolito Octaviano Jr.
  • Synchronous: Saturday, 8am-10am | Asynchronous: Sunday

The Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) course introduces students to the foundational principles and practices of designing interactive systems that effectively support human use. It explores key topics such as interaction design basics, design rules, and evaluation techniques, with a strong emphasis on understanding HCI paradigms and integrating them into the software development process. The course is structured around lectures, asynchronous activities, and a hands-on project, allowing students to apply theoretical knowledge to practical design challenges.

A major component of the course is a team-based project in which students conceptualize and design a smartphone application that provides audio-based navigation assistance for visually impaired users. This project includes market analysis, user interface design, and an implementation plan, culminating in a final presentation. Through this project, students gain experience in accessible design, user-centered development, and real-world problem solving, reinforcing the course's goal of creating inclusive and effective interactive systems.

Course objectives

  • Understand core HCI principles and practices
  • Design user-centered interactive system prototypes
  • Apply accessibility in interface development
